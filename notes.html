Notes for Undergraduate Courses

Course Notes

I wrote the following notes to supplement undergraduate courses that I taught at the University of Washington and Stony Brook. I give permission for instructors to use them in their own classes and for individuals to use them for personal study.

Undergraduate Courses

Integral Calculus

Mean Value Property for Linear Functions - April 17, 2012
These are supplemental notes for a discussion about the "average value" of a function. The goal is to show students that the averages of a linear functions have a very special property.

Differential Equations

Harmonic Functions - November 29, 2010
We look at examples of harmonic functions and two of their important properties: the mean value property and the maximum principle.

Linear Algebra

Modified Gram-Schmidt Algorithm - February 24, 2010
We show how to find an orthonormal set of vectors.
Four Fundamental Subspaces - February 5, 2010
We show how to find a basis for each of the four fundamental subsapces of a matrix. Then we discuss the Fundamental Theorem of Linear Algebra, Part I (the rank-nullity law).
Basis of a Vector Space - February 3, 2010
We record the proof that every vector subspace of Rn has a basis and every basis has the same number of elements.
LU Factorization - January 13, 2010
We describe the beautiful LU factorization of a square matrix (or how to write Gaussian elimination in terms of matrix multiplication).
Gaussian Elimination - January 6, 2010
We outline the method of forward elinimation and back-substitution to find solutions of n linear equations in n unknowns.
Geometry of Linear Equations
We describe the "row picture" and "column picture" of a system of linear equations.
